About

I grew up curious and was always a thinker. Looking back, it all makes sense now - but it didn't always didn't seem that way. While immersed in Research in Biological Sciences at University of Chicago at the age of 15, I was passionate in observing the world at a microscopic level while honing the art of sequencing genetic material. At the time, it was truly a monumental experience for me to work alongside individuals that were prominent figures for the Human Genome Project. My continued curiousities and passion in sciences led me to University of Illinois at Chicago. I entered a Pre-Med program with focus in Biochemistry, which allowed me to continue fostering my growth in research and analytics. I found myself manipulating amino acid seqences of various bacterial strains to help discover antibiotic treatments effective for antibiotic-resistant strains (a common example is MRSA). I also actively worked on anti-angiogenic therapy research for battling against breast cancer and proudly published articles in a pre-health campus journal. Upon graduation day, I had a funny feeling that a different version of myself was starting to grow out of my shell.

Uh-oh. A more curious version of me that yearned to venture further out and outside the laboratory environments had emerged.

I decided that I was no longer decided on a path to medicine. Deep down, I felt confused as to whether my purpose was to become a doctor or to earn my parents' love by satisfying their wishes for me to pursue that 'Dr' title. Sure enough, I found a way to test that hypothesis and found myself at a vigorous medical practice. I was now medical assistant and clinical researcher for off-label usage of medications. I enjoyed charting patient notes, assisting with skin cancer exams and surguries in the OR, e-scribing medications with doctor supervision, working with insurance companies for billing, and publishing papers with some of the most highly respected names in the area of Allergic Contact Dermatitis. One of the coolest projects was compiling data scraped for millions of products off sites to compile a massive reference spreadsheet that patients would later utlize as their 'bible' of items they could safely use or eat due to their medical conditions. After some time had passed, I realized a pattern of continuously hitting the wall with exhaustion and unhappy when burdened with cases where patients suffered. My level of empathy for patients just overwhelmed me with grief and feeling sadness for humans.

I took this as a sign to unplug myself. This was not for me.

I wasn't quite sure what I would do next, but I was quite sure of was that I'd rather die than stagnate on this journey called life. So onwards on this journey I went. Straight into what Tony Robbins described in his book, "Awaken the Giant Within". Sidenote: I became a huge fan of Tony Robbins, attended his conferences, and heck yes: I drank that Kool-Aid.

What started as an opportunity to help a small family retail business straighten out their inventory issues and better address theft issues soon had me absorb and engage in honing methodologies in buying merchandise and managing salesfloors. This included implementation of technological resources: installation of security cameras, correctly setting up computers and networking them correctly with internet and modem, vetting the right point of sales systems and software to track and streamline purchases, ensuring firewalls configurations kept employees from accessing "banned" websites, and getting saavy with B2B and B2C platforms for orders. I quickly found strategic efforts to maximize sales dollars earned per square footage of stores, embracing the data analytics skills acquired during many years of research. I self-learned to access and wrangle data with spreadsheets, graphs, and charts for predictive analysis and market forecasting. I worked closely with brands like Nike, Jordan, Adidas, Reebok, and more to continuously earn greater support as business needs continuously grew.

Until I got mad. REAL mad.

Hard earned profits were being dwindled away by credit card scammers - cases of identity theft, credit card chargeback disputes, notices from banks and payment card processors started to pile up. There was work to be done to better protect customer and payment data, on the customer-facing end and within the stores for cases involving store employees as well. Moreover, functionalities with store websites were impacted (from likely a big business competitor from what I was told) with what I later learned to be called cross-site scripting attacks. Shortly thereafter, my grandmother had just passed. A phone call from a childhood friend for condolences somehow led to a conversation on a small startup project in San Francisco.

I realized my growth was limited to my own environment. I uprooted myself from everyone and everything I had ever known. I was born and raised in Chicago, and never experienced living anywhere else. So I donated all my belongings that I could not fit into 2 small suitcases. It was time.

Since then, I have had the priviledge of being Data Security Manager and Secretary for the startup project before transitioning over to joining John Hancock Financial in Boston. This was a massive global enterprise, as John Hancock merged with Manulife Financial Corporation. Story to be continued....

Currently an active ISACA member, ISC2 member, LinkedIn admin for 2 groups [USA Patriot Act: Section 314(a) – FinCEN – Financial Crimes Enforcement Network, Data Loss Prevention (DLP)], Information Security mentor for students
